How to Calculate Concrete for a Slab, Footing, or Column

Ordering the right amount of concrete is critical — too little means a weak pour, too much is wasted money. Whether you're pouring a patio slab, a sidewalk, or setting fence post footings, here's how to estimate concrete quantities accurately.

Step 1: Measure Your Dimensions

For rectangular slabs (patios, driveways, sidewalks), measure the length, width, and thickness. A typical driveway slab is 4 inches thick, while a patio might be 4–6 inches. For columns or footings, measure the diameter and height.

Step 2: Calculate Volume in Cubic Feet

For slabs, multiply length × width × thickness (all in feet). For example, a 10×10 foot patio that's 4 inches thick: 10 × 10 × 0.33 = 33 cubic feet. For round columns, use the formula: π × radius² × height.

Step 3: Convert to Cubic Yards

Concrete is sold by the cubic yard. Divide your cubic feet by 27 to get cubic yards. That 10×10 patio would need about 1.22 cubic yards. Ready-mix trucks typically deliver a minimum of 1 yard.

Step 4: Account for Waste

Add 10% extra for uneven ground, form spillage, and slight variations in thickness. It's much cheaper to have a little extra than to order a second delivery — most concrete companies charge a short-load fee for small orders.

Bags vs. Ready-Mix

For small projects under 1 cubic yard, premixed bags (60 lb or 80 lb) are practical. An 80 lb bag fills about 0.6 cubic feet. For anything larger, order from a ready-mix truck — it's faster, stronger, and more cost-effective. Our calculator shows both the cubic yardage and the number of 80 lb bags needed.

Common Project Sizes

  • 10×10 slab (4 in thick) — about 1.2 cubic yards or 56 bags
  • 20×20 driveway (5 in thick) — about 6.2 cubic yards
  • Fence post hole (10 in × 36 in deep) — about 1.5 bags per hole
  • Sidewalk (3 ft × 30 ft × 4 in) — about 1.1 cubic yards

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bags of concrete do I need?
Divide your total cubic yards by the bag coverage. For example, 1 cubic yard needs about 45 bags of 80 lb concrete mix.
How thick should a concrete slab be?
Standard patios and walkways: 4 inches. Driveways: 5–6 inches. Garage floors: 6 inches minimum.
