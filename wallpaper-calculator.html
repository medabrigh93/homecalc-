How to Measure a Room for Wallpaper

Figuring out how many rolls of wallpaper you need requires careful measurement. Unlike paint, wallpaper comes in fixed-width rolls with pattern repeats that affect how much you actually use. Here's how to measure correctly.

Step 1: Measure Wall Height and Perimeter

Measure the height of your walls from floor to ceiling. Then measure the total perimeter of the room — the combined length of all walls. For a 12×14 foot room, the perimeter is 52 feet (12 + 14 + 12 + 14).

Step 2: Calculate Total Wall Area

Multiply the wall height by the total perimeter. An 8-foot ceiling in a room with a 52-foot perimeter gives you 416 square feet of wall space.

Step 3: Subtract Doors and Windows

Subtract the area of all doors (about 21 sq ft each) and windows (about 12 sq ft each). With two doors and three windows, you'd subtract about 78 sq ft, leaving 338 sq ft of wallpaper area.

Step 4: Check Your Roll Specifications

A standard American single roll covers about 36 square feet (but only about 28–30 sq ft after trimming and waste). European rolls may differ. Check the label for the roll width, length, and pattern repeat distance.

Step 5: Account for Pattern Repeats

Patterned wallpaper requires matching at the seams, which means more waste. A large pattern repeat (12+ inches) can waste 15–25% more wallpaper compared to a solid or small pattern. Always round up to the next full roll — you can't buy partial rolls.

Pro Tips

  • Order all rolls from the same batch/run number to guarantee color consistency
  • Buy 1–2 extra rolls for mistakes, future repairs, and pattern matching
  • Textured walls may need lining paper first, which adds to material costs
  • Vinyl and peel-and-stick wallpapers are easier to install for DIY beginners

How to Calculate Wallpaper

Wall Perimeter = 2 × (Length + Width)

Wall Area = Perimeter × Height − (Doors × 21 sq ft) − (Windows × 15 sq ft)

Usable area per roll = Roll Width (ft) × Roll Length (ft)

Rolls = Wall Area ÷ Usable Area per Roll (rounded up)

Standard Roll Sizes

  • American Single Roll: 20.5" wide × 33 ft long ≈ 56 sq ft
  • Euro Roll: 21" wide × 33 ft long ≈ 57.75 sq ft
  • Wallpaper usually sold in double rolls — check your supplier

Frequently Asked Questions

How many rolls of wallpaper for a 12×12 room?
With 8 ft ceilings and standard rolls (20.5" × 33'), you'd need about 7–8 single rolls after subtracting doors and windows.
Should I buy extra rolls?
Yes — buy 1–2 extra rolls to account for pattern matching, mistakes, and future repairs. Dye lots can vary between batches.
