How to Calculate Concrete

Slab: Volume = Length × Width × Depth (all in feet), then divide by 27 to get cubic yards.

Column: Volume = π × (Radius²) × Depth (all in feet), then divide by 27.

Concrete Bag Coverage

  • 40 lb bag — covers about 0.011 cubic yards
  • 60 lb bag — covers about 0.017 cubic yards
  • 80 lb bag — covers about 0.022 cubic yards

For projects over 1 cubic yard, consider ordering ready-mix concrete from a local supplier.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bags of concrete do I need?
Divide your total cubic yards by the bag coverage. For example, 1 cubic yard needs about 45 bags of 80 lb concrete mix.
How thick should a concrete slab be?
Standard patios and walkways: 4 inches. Driveways: 5–6 inches. Garage floors: 6 inches minimum.
