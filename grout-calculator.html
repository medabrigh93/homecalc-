How Grout Is Calculated

Grout volume depends on joint width, tile thickness, and the number of joints per square foot.

The formula calculates the total linear inches of grout joints per tile, multiplied by joint cross-section area, then scaled to your total area.

Common Joint Widths

  • 1/16" (0.0625") — Rectified/minimal grout lines
  • 1/8" (0.125") — Standard for wall tiles
  • 3/16" (0.1875") — Standard for floor tiles
  • 1/4" (0.25") — Natural stone, rustic look

Frequently Asked Questions

How much grout for 100 sq ft of 12×12 tile?
With standard 1/8" joints and 3/8" thick tile, you'll need approximately 6–8 lbs of sanded grout for 100 sq ft.
How many sq ft does a 25 lb bag of grout cover?
It varies widely by tile size and joint width. For 12×12 tiles with 1/8" joints, a 25 lb bag covers roughly 300–400 sq ft.
