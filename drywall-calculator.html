How to Calculate Drywall

Wall Area = 2 × (Length + Width) × Height

Ceiling Area = Length × Width (if included)

Net Area = Total − (Doors × 21 sq ft) − (Windows × 15 sq ft)

Sheets = Net Area ÷ Sheet Size (rounded up, +10% waste)

Drywall Thickness Guide

  • 1/4" — Curved walls, covering old surfaces
  • 3/8" — Patches and repairs
  • 1/2" — Standard walls and ceilings
  • 5/8" — Fire-rated, soundproofing, garage ceilings

Don't Forget

  • Joint compound: ~1 gallon per 100 sq ft
  • Joint tape: ~1 roll per 300 sq ft
  • Screws: ~32 screws per 4×8 sheet

Frequently Asked Questions

How many sheets of drywall for a 12×12 room?
Walls only (8 ft ceiling): about 12 sheets of 4×8. Including ceiling: about 17 sheets.
Should I use 4×8 or 4×12 sheets?
4×12 sheets create fewer joints (cleaner finish) but are heavier and harder to handle. Use 4×8 for DIY and 4×12 for professional installs.
