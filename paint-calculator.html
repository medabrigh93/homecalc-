How to Calculate Paint Needed

To calculate how much paint you need, our calculator uses this formula:

Wall Area = 2 × (Length + Width) × Height

Paintable Area = Wall Area − (Doors × 21 sq ft) − (Windows × 15 sq ft)

Gallons = (Paintable Area × Coats) ÷ Coverage per Gallon

Tips for Accurate Results

  • Standard door opening is about 21 sq ft (3×7 ft)
  • Average window is about 15 sq ft (3×5 ft)
  • Most paints cover 350-400 sq ft per gallon on smooth surfaces
  • Textured walls or dark-to-light color changes may need an extra coat
  • Always round up and buy a little extra for touch-ups

Frequently Asked Questions

How much paint do I need for a 12×12 room?
A typical 12×12 room with 8-foot ceilings has about 384 sq ft of wall area. After subtracting a door and two windows, you'll need roughly 1 gallon for one coat or 2 gallons for two coats.
How much does 1 gallon of paint cover?
One gallon typically covers 350–400 square feet on smooth surfaces. Textured, porous, or unpainted surfaces may need more paint.
Should I buy extra paint?
Yes. We recommend buying 10–15% extra for touch-ups, waste, and uneven surfaces. Our calculator already factors this in.
