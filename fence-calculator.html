How to Calculate Fence Materials

Building a fence is one of the most common DIY projects, but buying the right amount of materials is the difference between a smooth build and multiple trips to the lumber yard. Here's how to plan your fence project.

Step 1: Measure Your Fence Line

Walk the entire fence line with a tape measure or measuring wheel. Mark the corners and the locations of any gates. Note the total linear feet — this is the most important number for calculating materials.

Step 2: Calculate Posts

Fence posts are typically spaced 6 or 8 feet apart (center to center). Divide your total fence length by the post spacing, then add 1 for the end post. A 100-foot fence with 8-foot spacing needs 14 posts (100 ÷ 8 + 1 = 13.5, rounded up to 14).

Step 3: Calculate Rails

Most fences use 2 horizontal rails (top and bottom), though fences over 6 feet tall should have 3 rails. Each section between posts gets its own set of rails. For 13 sections with 2 rails each, you need 26 rails.

Step 4: Calculate Pickets

To calculate pickets, you need to know the picket width and the gap between them. Standard pickets are 3.5 inches wide with a 1.75-inch gap. Divide the total fence length (minus posts and gates) by the picket width plus gap, then round up.

Step 5: Don't Forget These Items

  • Post caps — one per post, protects against water damage
  • Concrete — 1–2 bags of 80 lb concrete per post hole (dig 1/3 of total post length deep)
  • Gate hardware — hinges, latch, and a heavier gate post
  • Screws or nails — about 8 screws per picket (4 per rail) plus rail fasteners

Fence Height Guide

  • 3–4 feet — decorative or front yard fences, garden borders
  • 6 feet — standard privacy fence (most common)
  • 8 feet — maximum height in most jurisdictions; check local codes

Important: Always check your local building codes and HOA rules before building. Many areas require permits for fences over 6 feet and setbacks from property lines.

Fence Material Guide

Post Spacing

  • 6 ft — Heavy-duty, high wind areas
  • 8 ft — Standard for most fences
  • 10 ft — Light-duty, decorative fences

Common Picket Sizes

  • 1×4 (3.5") — Standard dog-ear picket
  • 1×6 (5.5") — Privacy fence boards
  • No gap — Full privacy fence
  • 1-2" gap — Semi-private / decorative

Frequently Asked Questions

How many fence posts for 100 feet of fence?
With standard 8 ft spacing, you need 14 posts (100 ÷ 8 = 12.5 sections, rounded up = 13 sections + 1 end post = 14).
How deep should fence posts be?
Generally 1/3 of total post length underground. For a 6 ft fence, use 8 ft posts with 2 ft buried.
