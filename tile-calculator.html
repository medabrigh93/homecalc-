How to Calculate Tiles Needed

Area = Length × Width (in sq ft)

Tile Area = Tile Width × Tile Height (converted to sq ft)

Tiles Needed = (Area ÷ Tile Area) × (1 + Waste %)

Common Tile Sizes

  • 12×12 in — 1 sq ft per tile, most popular for floors
  • 6×6 in — 0.25 sq ft per tile, common for walls & backsplashes
  • 24×24 in — 4 sq ft per tile, large format floors
  • 3×6 in — subway tile, popular for kitchen backsplashes

Frequently Asked Questions

How many tiles do I need per square foot?
It depends on tile size. For 12×12 inch tiles you need 1 tile per sq ft. For 6×6 inch tiles, you need 4 per sq ft. Our calculator handles any size.
How much extra tile should I buy?
Buy 10–15% extra for cuts, breakage, and waste. Complex patterns or diagonal layouts may need 15–20% extra.
