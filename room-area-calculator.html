How to Calculate Square Footage of Any Room

Knowing the exact square footage of a room is the first step in almost every home improvement project — from ordering flooring to estimating paint. Here's how to measure any room shape accurately.

Rectangular Rooms

The simplest case: measure the length and width in feet, then multiply. A 12×15 foot bedroom is 180 square feet. Always measure at the widest points and at floor level, not at eye height where walls might bow slightly.

L-Shaped and Irregular Rooms

Break the room into rectangles. An L-shaped living room can be divided into two rectangles — measure each one separately and add the areas. For example, if one section is 10×12 (120 sq ft) and the extension is 8×6 (48 sq ft), the total is 168 sq ft.

Rooms with Alcoves and Closets

Decide whether to include alcoves, bay windows, and walk-in closets in your measurement. For flooring, include every area that gets covered. For paint, you'll measure wall area instead of floor area.

Converting Between Units

  • Inches to feet: divide by 12 (e.g., 144 inches = 12 feet)
  • Square feet to square yards: divide by 9 (e.g., 180 sq ft = 20 sq yards)
  • Square feet to square meters: multiply by 0.0929 (e.g., 180 sq ft = 16.7 sq m)

Measure Twice, Order Once

Small measurement errors add up fast. A room that's actually 12.5 feet wide instead of 12 feet adds 7.5 extra square feet over a 15-foot length. That's enough to throw off material estimates. Use a quality 25-foot tape measure and have someone help hold the other end for accuracy.

When You Need Square Footage

  • Buying flooring, carpet, or tile
  • Estimating paint quantities (measure wall area, not floor)
  • Comparing room sizes or planning furniture layout
  • Calculating heating/cooling capacity (BTU requirements)
  • Real estate listings and appraisals

How to Measure Room Square Footage

For a simple rectangular room: Area = Length × Width

For L-shaped or irregular rooms, divide the room into rectangles, measure each one, and add them together. Our calculator lets you add multiple sections to handle any shape.

Tips

  • Measure in feet (convert inches to decimals: 6 inches = 0.5 ft)
  • For closets and alcoves, add them as separate sections
  • Round up to the nearest 0.5 ft when measuring

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate square footage of an L-shaped room?
Divide the L into two rectangles. Measure length × width of each, then add them. Use the "+ Add Section" button above to enter both.
How many square feet is a 10×12 room?
120 square feet (10 × 12 = 120).
